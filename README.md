# mroux

`mroux.net` is my public engineering notebook and professional identity layer.

It exists to present the deeper version of my work that does not fit cleanly into a resume, LinkedIn profile, or raw GitHub repositories.

## Why This Site Exists

This site is meant to be useful over many years, not just during a job search cycle.

It helps answer:

- what kind of engineer I am
- what systems and problems I care about
- how I think through tradeoffs
- what I have built, measured, and written

## What You Can Find Here

The site is organized into a small set of durable sections:

- `About`: professional context and focus areas
- `Projects`: selected engineering work with artifacts and rationale
- `Writing`: technical articles, implementation notes, and reflections
- `Systems`: design notes and reliability-oriented thinking
- `Benchmarks`: methodology and performance experiment summaries
- `Stats`: lightweight, public summary metrics about published content
- `Resume`: application-friendly resume access point

## Principles

The content and structure emphasize:

- clarity over promotion
- evidence over claims
- concise writing over marketing language
- long-term maintainability over short-term novelty

The goal is simple: a credible, readable, and useful technical home on the web.
